Data Analysis

Children: Suffered from distress and isolation due to school closures and loss of social activities. Increased screen time and negative social media exposure contributed to mental health challenges.

Adolescents: Experienced anxiety, depression, and loneliness due to school disruptions and lack of social interactions. Concerns about academic and career goals were prevalent.

Adults: Faced anxiety, job loss, and work-from-home stress. The blurred distinction between work and personal life led to mental health struggles, including burnout.

Elderly: Suffered from social exclusion, isolation, and health fears due to lockdowns. Increased anxiety and loneliness significantly impacted their well-being.

All age groups experienced loneliness, isolation, and anxiety, with unique challenges for each. Understanding these mental health impacts provides a comprehensive view of the pandemic’s effects on different demographics.
